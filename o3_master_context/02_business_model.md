# 02 Business Model

## Target Customers

Primary target:

- Thai SMEs and growing companies with approximately 50–500 employees.
- Companies that do not have strong HR analytics capability.
- Companies that rely heavily on Excel, payroll exports, or fragmented HR files.
- Business owners, executives, HR managers, and HR transformation teams.

Initial priority industries:

1. Manufacturing
2. Trading
3. Service

Future industries:

- Retail
- Logistics
- Healthcare
- Construction
- Hospitality
- Education
- State-owned or enterprise organizations

## Core Customer Problems

1. They have data but cannot interpret it.
2. HR reports are too operational and not strategic.
3. Dashboard tools are too complex or require clean data.
4. AI tools lack company context.
5. SME owners want answers, not analytics jargon.
6. HR teams need productivity tools but do not know how to prompt AI effectively.
7. Benchmark data for Thai SMEs is limited.

## Value Proposition

O³ ZONE delivers value through:

- Fast workforce understanding.
- AI-generated insight and action plans.
- Practical HR tools.
- Simple data template instead of complex integration.
- Executive-friendly dashboard.
- Future benchmark comparison.
- Academy content that converts users into paying customers.

## Revenue Model

The business model should combine subscription, credits, services, and future benchmark revenue.

### Subscription Packages

Recommended package naming:

1. **Basic** = Free
2. **Starter**
3. **Professional**
4. **Business**
5. **Enterprise**

### Revenue Streams

- Monthly or annual subscription.
- AI usage credits.
- Paid Academy courses.
- Setup and onboarding service.
- Custom dashboard and consulting.
- Enterprise integration fee.
- Benchmark premium access in future.
- Marketplace or template store in future.

## Freemium Strategy

Freemium should not give everything away. It should create a clear path from curiosity to value.

Basic users should get:

- Limited assessment.
- Limited AI insight.
- Sample dashboard.
- Basic data upload template.
- Free Academy courses.
- Limited AI credits.

Paid users get:

- Full upload and validation.
- More dashboards.
- AI Advisor.
- Export reports.
- More AI tools.
- More credits.
- Advanced action plans.
- Benchmark access when available.

## Academy Role

Academy should be treated primarily as a lead generation and customer education engine, not the main revenue source at the beginning.

Academy should:

- Teach data-driven HR.
- Teach AI for executives.
- Teach ChatGPT/Claude for HR.
- Lead learners into O³ products.
- Provide prompts, templates, and mini workshops.

## CRM Strategy

CRM should collect:

- User profile
- Company profile
- Industry
- Employee size
- Lead source
- Product usage
- AI usage
- Course progress
- Assessment completion
- Upload attempts
- Subscription status

CRM should help answer:

- Which users are activated?
- Which product triggers conversion?
- Which industry has the highest adoption?
- Which feature creates paid conversion?
- Which leads should be contacted by sales?

## Sales Motion

Recommended go-to-market path:

1. Free content and Academy.
2. Free assessment or basic upload.
3. AI-generated insight report.
4. Upgrade to paid dashboard or AI tools.
5. Setup service for companies that need help.
6. Retention through recurring insight and action tracking.
